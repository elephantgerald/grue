### Important

**[IMPORTANT] Scope gap: trophy case deposit scoring not wired — gverbs.zil:1113**

The issue explicitly calls out three scoring events. Only one (collecting) is wired. ZIL `gverbs.zil:1113` shows `<SCORE-OBJ ,PRSO>` called in `V-PUT` — this is how depositing a treasure in the trophy case awards points. The Clojure `v-put` equivalent has no `score-obj` call. Treasures deposited in the trophy case will silently yield no points at runtime.

The PR closes #6 entirely, but two of the three stated scoring events remain unwired. Recommend either: (a) keep #6 open and document what's deferred, or (b) create a follow-up issue before closing.

---

**[IMPORTANT] `score-upd` missing the 350-point endgame side effect — gverbs.zil:1855–1866**

ZIL `SCORE-UPD` does more than increment atoms. When `SCORE` reaches 350 and `WON-FLAG` is false, it fires:
- Sets `WON-FLAG T`
- Clears `MAP`'s `:invisible` flag (unlocks the endgame map item)
- Clears `WEST-OF-HOUSE`'s `:touchbit` flag (resets entry to the opening area)
- Prints: `"An almost inaudible voice whispers in your ear, \"Look to your treasures for the final secret.\""`

The Clojure `score-upd` at `actions.clj` simply increments both atoms and returns. A player who reaches 350 points will get no whisper, WON-FLAG will never be set, and the MAP item remains invisible. This is an observable correctness deviation at a high-stakes moment.

This likely requires `won-flag` and room state infrastructure not yet in place; the right fix may be a follow-up issue rather than blocking this PR. But it should be acknowledged.

---

**[IMPORTANT] Room-entry scoring not wired — gverbs.zil:2122**

ZIL `V-WALK` calls `<SCORE-OBJ .RM>` when entering a room (line 2122). Several rooms have a `:value` property and award points on first entry. The Clojure `v-walk` equivalent has no `score-obj` call. Room-discovery scoring is completely absent.

### Suggestions

**[SUGGESTION] `v-score` return value diverges from ZIL**

ZIL `V-SCORE` returns `,SCORE` (the score integer, per line 4044 of `1actions.zil`). The Clojure `v-score` returns `nil` implicitly. This has no impact in the current call paths (return value discarded), but it's a silent divergence from ZIL semantics that could matter if `v-score` is ever composed.

**[SUGGESTION] Death score penalty not wired**

ZIL `JIGS-UP` calls `<SCORE-UPD -10>` (the death penalty). This is out of scope for this PR but is the natural next step once `v-score` is wired — worth filing as a follow-up so it doesn't get lost.

**[SUGGESTION] `score-command-format-at-zero` does not assert exact output**

The test uses `clojure.string/includes?` throughout. For the V-SCORE format test this is fine (matches ZIL verification practice). Consider whether a single `=` assertion on the full two-line output would be more robust for the exact-format case per CLAUDE.md's policy of trusting DOSBox-verified output.

## Overall Assessment

The core infrastructure — `score`/`base-score` atoms, `score-upd`, `score-obj`, `v-score` rank table, and `v-take` wiring — is correctly implemented and faithfully mirrors the ZIL. The rank table thresholds match the ZIL source exactly. The 20 tests are solid.

However, **the PR closes #6 prematurely.** Two of the three stated scoring events (trophy case deposit, room discovery) are not wired, and `score-upd` is missing its endgame side effect. The practical impact is: a player will not receive points for depositing treasures, will not receive points for discovering rooms, and will not hear the endgame whisper at 350 points.

**Recommended course of action:** Request the author either keep #6 open and scope the PR to "v-take scoring only," or create follow-up issues for the remaining wiring before closing #6.
